About Me

Peethadhishwar Mahant Bharat Bhushan Joshi is the head priest (Peethadhishwar) of the sacred temple Rudrani Mahakalika Shaktipeeth. With a lifetime dedicated to spirituality since childhood, he has received the company and blessings of various saints and sages, walking the noble path of dharma.

In 2002, he was honored with the revered title of Peethadhishwar Mahant and was given the sacred seat (Gadi). For the past 24 years, Maharaj has been performing devoted worship of Mahakalika Ji during the auspicious Navratri season, observing complete fasts, vows of silence, barefoot prayers, and early morning rituals at 4:00 AM during Brahma Muhurta.

Famous for his accurate astrological predictions, Maharaj continuously preaches about collecting one roti (bread) from every household for Gau Mata (Mother Cow), emphasizing that "Parents are the world's greatest heroes," and advocating for Kanya Bachao Kanya Padhao (Save the Girl Child, Educate the Girl Child) and water conservation.

Being proficient in Yoga Vidya (the science of yoga), Maharaj provides yoga education to various schools, police departments, colleges, and corporate organizations. He dedicates his days to the education of girls and works tirelessly to uplift society. Distinguished people from all walks of life meet Maharaj daily to receive his divine guidance and blessings.
